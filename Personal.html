Linguistics: Semantics Review paper on Natural Logic in Natural Language Inference (NLI)

In fall, 2017, I took a graduate course Semantics 1 with professor Anna Szabolsci, at NYU. As the final project of the class I produced a review paper of the use of Natural Logic in Natural Language Inference (NLI), largely drawing from Bill MacCartney's 2009 thesis (Natural Language Inference) and Ellie Pavlick's 2017 thesis (Compositional Lexical Semantics in Natural Language Inference). I also drew from Larry Moss's work on proof theoretic approaches, as well as other miscellaneous resources (notably Lauri Karttunen's "Natural Logic to Natural Reasoning") on rule-based inference based approaches. Here is my review paper and my presentation.
